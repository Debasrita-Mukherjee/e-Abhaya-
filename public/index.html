Legal aid & Indian laws

Fundamental Right Right #1

Right to Free Legal Aid

Every woman in India has a fundamental right to free legal services under Article 39A of the Indian Constitution, regardless of her income. If a woman cannot afford a lawyer, the Legal Services Authority will appoint one for her free of cost.

Legal Provision: Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987
Arrest Protection Right #2

The Night Arrest Protection

A woman cannot be arrested after sunset (6 PM) and before sunrise (6 AM), except in exceptional circumstances. Even in such rare cases, the arrest must be made by a woman officer, and prior permission from a Judicial Magistrate must be obtained.

Legal Provision: Section 46(4) of Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC) / BNSS
LODGING PROTECTION Right #3

Right to File a Zero FIR

A woman can lodge an FIR at *any* police station, irrespective of where the crime occurred or jurisdiction boundaries. The police station is legally bound to accept the complaint, register it under a "Zero" serial number, and immediately transfer it to the jurisdictional station.

Legal Provision: Supreme Court Ruling & Ministry of Home Affairs Advisory
Privacy Shield Right #4

Right to Anonymity and Privacy

It is a criminal offense to publish or disclose the name or any details that reveal the identity of a victim of sexual assault or harassment. A woman has the right to record her statement privately in the presence of a woman police officer or a magistrate.

Legal Provision: Section 72 of BNS / Section 228A of IPC
Domestic Abuse BNS Section 85 / 86

Cruelty by Husband or Relatives (Section 498A IPC)

Protects married women from physical, mental, or emotional cruelty, harassment, or torture by their husband or his relatives. This includes persistent demands for dowry, coercion, or severe mental distress. The offense is non-bailable.

Punishment: Imprisonment up to 3 years and a heavy fine
Harassment BNS Section 74 / 75

Assault & Sexual Harassment (Section 354 IPC)

Protects women against any acts of physical force, unwanted physical contact, demands for sexual favors, showing pornography against consent, or making sexually colored remarks. Outraging the modesty of a woman is heavily penalized.

Punishment: Rigorous imprisonment from 1 to 5 years with fines
Monitoring & Stalking BNS Section 78

Stalking and Cyberstalking (Section 354D IPC)

It is a crime to follow a woman, contact her repeatedly, or monitor her internet usage, social media accounts, emails, or other digital communication despite a clear indication of disinterest. This includes online harassment and digital tracking.

Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment for first offense, 5 years for subsequent
Special Act PWDVA, 2005

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

A comprehensive civil act protecting women from physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse within a shared household. It provides rights for protection orders, residence orders, monetary relief, custody orders, and free medical care.

Provision: Protection Orders, Monetary Relief, Free Residence Rights
Special Act POSH Act, 2013

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (POSH) Act

Mandates every organization with 10 or more employees to set up an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) to address sexual harassment. It defines safe workspaces and provides legal resolution mechanics for working women in both formal and informal sectors.

Provision: Internal Complaints Committee, Safe Workspace Guidelines
